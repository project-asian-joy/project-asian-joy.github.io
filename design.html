My Works

Embracing creative placemaking, I use culture-specific imagery to signal my community, redefine spaces and presence within underheard communities, and reclaim the struggles of not being recognized. I look to discover my identity within the truths and fictions of culture-making, asking the question: What is remembered and what is lost?

My work challenges contemporary art's definition by leveraging non-traditional forms of art and creative technology as a platform for underheard communities to tell their own stories, on their own terms, including my own.

Drawing from my experience living in Boston Chinatown, where access to green spaces is scarce, my work exudes a longing for connection with nature and invites viewers to reconnect with the beauty and wonder of the world that surrounds us, even in the heart of the city. Below I highlight some representative works.

Celebrating Culture and Place

Performing Arts -
Chinese Lion Dance

I have 10+ years experience performing with an all-Asian women lion dance troupe to not only celebrate and preserve my cultural heritage but also pave the way for a new tradition in an art form traditionally denied to women. I play the buddha in this video.
Photo - Lines

Each line—from the sharp angles of signs, windows, and buildings to the visual cues on the streets to the orderly procession of students crossing one of the busiest intersections in Chinatown—leads the viewer's gaze through a maze-like visual narrative.
Photo - Chinese Lion Heads

This photograph of a family of Chinese lion heads meticulously arranged captures not only the physical beauty of the lion heads, but also the spirit of unity, resilience, and ancestral pride that they represent.
Printmaking - Hong Baos

The tactile nature of this art combines craftmanship with tradition. Each print bears the marks of the artist's hand to convey hearfelt wishes for the recipient.
3D Modeling and Animation -
Imperial Chinese Garden

I experimented using OpenGL to render a model of an imperial Chinese garden in Suzhou and animated the camera motion to pan the screen.
Graphic Design/Photo -
Looking At You

I wanted to transform a photo by using graphic design to convey a sense of rawness and mystery, inviting viewers to question what details I may have added and left out.

Amplifying Underheard Stories

Visual Novel - Control Alt Delete

This Choose-Your-Own-Adventure style storygame is named after a nonprofit organization called Control Alt Delete, which helps people escape domestic violence. Through interactive storytelling, players are put in the shoes of someone trying to reset their life.
Interactive Story - Problama Llama

This game features a Latina woman as the protagonist helping her neighbor find and bring home a mischievous llama. It introduces players to Peruvian culture while teaching code in fun, easy, interactive way.
Newspaper Article - Immigrant History Trail

I wrote an article about a project I contributed to, uplifting Boston Chinatown's cultural legacy.
