Bakeries

Celebrate life’s sweetest moments at May’s Cake House, where the mango mousse cake, bursting with fresh fruit and the perfect touch of sweetness, is a birthday favorite. Students pick out delightful treats for their teachers, honoring a cherished tradition, while families enjoy comforting lo mai fan (steamed sticky rice wrapped in lotus leaves) and rich durian cheesecake.

Step into Ho Yuen Bakery, where generations find nostalgia and delight. From crispy, sweet pineapple buns to delectable red bean cakes, lotus mooncakes, and egg custard tarts, every bite is a taste of tradition. Come see why locals keep coming back for more!

Restaurants

Experience Winsor Dim Sum Cafe, where every dish is made-to-order and served with a smile. Delight in fluffy steamed pork buns, crispy stuffed eggplant, and perfectly charred pan-fried turnip cakes, while sharing a tradition that brings generations together around the table.

For the curious and adventurous, try Taiwan Cafe's crispy stinky tofu, with its crispy exterior and melt-in-your-mouth center, served with tangy pickled cabbage for an authentic Taiwanese delight. People also rave about their spicy beef noodle soup. Each visit is filled with fond memories of laughter and shared meals, making it a perfect spot for new stories and fun dining experiences.

For a hands-down satisfying take-out meal, head to Chinatown Cafe. Their Sam Bo Fan, or "Three Treasures Rice," brims with tender chicken, flavorful char siu, and savory roasted duck where each box is packed so generously that they use a rubber band to keep it together.

Shops

Uncover the secrets of health at Nam Bac Hong Chinese Herbs on Harrison or Gar Lok Herbal Store on Tyler, where the soothing scent of herbal remedies greets you at the door. Browse through their shelves full of jars of dried herbs and aromatic teas, each offering a glimpse into traditional healing practices.

For everything you need or didn’t know you needed, Essex Corner is your go-to. From beautiful paper lanterns and red envelopes to unique decor, jewelry, and home goods, plus whimsical toys, stylish accessories, and adorable stationery, this cozy shop is a treasure trove of charming finds.

The Thousand Bloom

The Thousand Bloom is a public art project featuring 1,000 silk chrysanthemums to symbolize Boston Chinatown’s resilience, incorporating a grand installation, business support initiatives, and Chinatown’s first ever Double Ninth Festival.
